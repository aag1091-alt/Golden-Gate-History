Irving Foster Morrow, born on September 22, 1884, and died on October 28, 1952, was an American architect best known for his influential role in the visual design of the Golden Gate Bridge, significantly enhancing its aesthetic appeal and integrating it beautifully into the San Francisco Bay Area landscape.

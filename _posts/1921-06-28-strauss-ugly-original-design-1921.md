# The Golden Gate Bridge's Ugly Origins

In 1921, Joseph Strauss, the chief engineer of the Golden Gate Bridge project, unveiled his initial design, which was widely criticized as an 'upside-down rat trap.' [1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Gate_Bridge)

**What happened:** In 1921, Joseph Strauss proposed the first design for the Golden Gate Bridge, which was met with harsh criticism for its industrial appearance. Over the next decade, the design underwent significant revisions, primarily by engineer Charles Ellis and architect Irving Morrow, transforming it into the iconic Art Deco structure we recognize today. [1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Gate_Bridge) [2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Strauss_(engineer)) [3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Alton_Ellis)

**Why it matters:** This episode underscores the collaborative nature of architectural and engineering projects and highlights the importance of design refinement. The Golden Gate Bridge's aesthetic transformation not only improved its visual appeal but also cemented its status as a global landmark.

# The Road Zipper System: A Safety Milestone for the Golden Gate Bridge
In 2015, the Golden Gate Bridge underwent a critical safety enhancement with the installation of the Road Zipper system, a moveable median barrier designed to prevent head-on collisions.

**What happened:** Between 1970 and 2014, the Golden Gate Bridge experienced 16 fatal head-on collisions due to the lack of a physical median barrier. To address this issue, Denis Mulligan of Lindsay Construction led the project to install a $30 million Road Zipper system. This innovative solution involved a transfer machine that could shift 3,500 tons of concrete barrier segments, effectively creating a physical separation between opposing traffic lanes. [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Gate_Bridge) and [Golden Gate Bridge Highway and Transportation District](https://www.goldengate.org/bridge/history-research/bridge-construction/moveable-median-barrier/) provide detailed information on the project.

**Why it matters:** The Road Zipper system not only eliminated head-on crossover crashes but also allowed for flexible lane configurations during rush hour, accommodating varying traffic demands. This milestone significantly improved the safety of the Golden Gate Bridge, one of the most internationally recognized symbols of San Francisco and California.

# The Golden Gate Bridge: From Tragic Symbol to Lifeline

The Golden Gate Bridge, a symbol of San Francisco and an engineering marvel, became a tragic landmark for suicide attempts, with over 1,600 confirmed deaths since 1937. In 2014, the Golden Gate Bridge Board made a historic decision to approve a stainless steel net system to prevent suicides, marking a shift in public opinion from prioritizing aesthetics to valuing human life. This decision was spearheaded by activists like Denis Mulligan, Kevin Hines, and Mary Currie, who campaigned tirelessly for years to implement safety measures. The installation of the net, completed in 2023, has significantly reduced the number of suicides, transforming the bridge's identity from a symbol of despair to one of hope and compassion.

**Why it matters:** The unanimous vote in 2014 to install the suicide prevention barriers was a pivotal moment, demonstrating the power of advocacy and the importance of prioritizing human life over architectural aesthetics. The barriers have not only saved countless lives but also set a precedent for other iconic structures around the world.

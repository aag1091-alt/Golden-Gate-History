# The Golden Gate Bridge's Founding Myth: Pedestrian Day 1937

On May 27, 1937, an estimated 200,000 people walked across the newly opened Golden Gate Bridge, marking the beginning of a civic tradition that continues to this day. [Joseph Strauss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Gate_Bridge), the chief engineer, and [Miss Golden Gate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Gate_Bridge), the Fiesta Queen, led the celebration, which was a testament to the bridge's significance as a symbol of San Francisco and California. This event not only celebrated the bridge's opening but also established it as a cultural icon and a symbol of civic pride.

The pedestrian day in 1937 set the tone for the bridge's role as more than just a transportation link. It highlighted the bridge's importance as a public space and a symbol of community spirit, which continues to resonate today. The tradition of closing the bridge to cars on anniversaries and other special occasions has its roots in this historic day, reinforcing the bridge's status as a beloved landmark.

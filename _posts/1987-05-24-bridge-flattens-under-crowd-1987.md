On May 24, 1987, the Golden Gate Bridge faced a unique challenge during its 50th anniversary celebration when an estimated 300,000 people walked across its span, causing the roadway to flatten by nearly seven feet. [1] This event, known as "The Day the Bridge Went Flat," was a critical moment for the bridge's engineers, including Daniel E. Mohn and Arthur C. Elliott, who had never before tested the bridge under such a uniformly distributed load. [1]

This incident highlighted the importance of ongoing structural analysis and crowd-control measures for the Golden Gate Bridge. It underscored the need for continuous monitoring and adjustments to ensure the safety and integrity of the iconic structure. [1]

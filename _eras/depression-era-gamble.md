In the depths of the Great Depression, Bay Area residents voted to put up their homes, farms, and businesses as collateral for a $35 million bond issue — an extraordinary act of civic faith. Bank of America founder A.P. Giannini stepped in to buy the bonds when no other institution would. Behind the scenes, consulting engineer Charles Ellis quietly redesigned Strauss's clumsy original concept into the elegant suspension bridge we know today, only to be fired and written out of the story for decades.

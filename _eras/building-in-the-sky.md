period: "1933–1937"
---
Construction began on January 5, 1933, and the project became one of the most dangerous engineering feats of the twentieth century, with workers battling fog, wind, and currents hundreds of feet above the strait. A pioneering safety net strung beneath the bridge deck saved nineteen men who became known as the Halfway-to-Hell Club, though eleven workers died when a scaffold tore through the net in February 1937. The bridge opened on May 27, 1937, and approximately 200,000 pedestrians streamed across on its first day.

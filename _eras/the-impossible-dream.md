---
For decades, the idea of spanning the Golden Gate strait was dismissed as engineering fantasy. Railroad magnate Charles Crocker floated the concept as early as 1872, but the treacherous currents, deep water, and relentless fog convinced most experts it could never be done. The strait remained a barrier crossed only by ferry, shaping San Francisco's identity as a city bounded by water.

The bridge cemented its place in global popular culture through countless films, photographs, and postcards, becoming arguably the most photographed man-made structure on Earth. Yet a tragic counter-narrative emerged as the bridge gained notoriety as a site of suicide, sparking decades of anguished public debate over whether to install a barrier. Seismic vulnerability also entered the conversation as the 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake rattled the Bay Area and exposed risks in aging infrastructure.

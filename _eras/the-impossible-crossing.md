short_name: "The Impossible Crossing"
period: "1869–1916"
---
For decades, engineers and dreamers floated the idea of spanning the Golden Gate strait, but the treacherous currents, deep waters, and punishing fog made it seem impossible. Railroad magnates and early bridge advocates debated whether any structure could survive the strait's violent tidal forces. The idea simmered as San Francisco grew from a gold rush boomtown into a major metropolis hungry for connection to its northern hinterlands.

As traffic volumes exploded beyond anything the original designers imagined, engineers confronted alarming structural realities — the bridge's roadway deck was never built for the loads it now carried daily. The fiftieth anniversary celebration in 1987 drew so many pedestrians that the bridge deck visibly flattened under their weight, a moment that shocked engineers and accelerated seismic retrofit planning. The 1989 Loma Prieta earthquake, which collapsed a section of the Bay Bridge, turned Golden Gate seismic vulnerability from an engineering concern into an urgent public crisis.

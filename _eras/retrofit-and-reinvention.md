A massive, multi-decade seismic retrofit program replaced the bridge's approach viaducts and strengthened its towers and cables to withstand a magnitude 8.3 earthquake, costing over $400 million in total. The district introduced electronic tolling, movable median barriers to prevent head-on collisions, and began serious consideration of a suicide deterrent system after decades of painful public debate. The bridge's eightieth anniversary found it safer and stronger than ever, yet still wrestling with its dual identity as an engineering marvel and a site of profound human suffering.

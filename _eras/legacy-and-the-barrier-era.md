After decades of debate and controversy, construction of a stainless steel suicide deterrent net finally began, representing a profound shift in how the bridge district balanced its iconic aesthetics with public safety. The bridge confronted modern challenges including climate resilience, sustainable transportation, and its role in an era of remote work that reshaped commuter patterns. Approaching its centennial, the Golden Gate Bridge endures as both a working piece of infrastructure and an irreplaceable symbol of human daring.

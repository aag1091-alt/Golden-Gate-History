After more than seventy years of advocacy and anguish, construction of a stainless steel suicide deterrent net finally began, marking a historic shift in how the bridge confronts its darkest legacy. New challenges emerged as well — retrofit wind screens installed to protect cyclists unexpectedly caused the bridge to emit an eerie harmonic hum audible across the city in high winds, a phenomenon engineers scrambled to fix. As the bridge approaches its centennial, it remains a living structure, constantly maintained and reimagined to serve a region and a world that still sees it as one of humanity's greatest built achievements.

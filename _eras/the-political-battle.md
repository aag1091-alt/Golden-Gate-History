---
Joseph Strauss, a tenacious but little-known bridge engineer, launched a relentless campaign to convince skeptical politicians, hostile ferry operators, and wary taxpayers that a bridge across the Golden Gate was both feasible and necessary. He navigated brutal opposition from the Southern Pacific Railroad, the War Department, and rival engineers who mocked his early ungainly designs. The creation of the Golden Gate Bridge and Highway District in 1928 finally gave the project a legal and financial framework to move forward.

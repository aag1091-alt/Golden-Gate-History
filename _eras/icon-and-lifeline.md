The Golden Gate Bridge rapidly became the most photographed bridge on Earth, its International Orange towers emerging as the defining symbol of San Francisco and the American West. It transformed the economy of Marin and Sonoma counties, turning rural communities into commuter suburbs almost overnight. The bridge also became an unexpected site of tragedy, as suicides from the span began accumulating at a grim pace that would haunt the structure's legacy for generations.
